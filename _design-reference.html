Amara Osei is a Lagos-based painter whose practice sits at the intersection of West African visual traditions and contemporary abstraction. Her work explores themes of identity, memory, and belonging — rendered in rich ochres, deep umbers, and the occasional shock of cobalt.

Statement

My practice is rooted in the idea that objects hold memory. The vessels in my series are not containers — they are presences. They sit, they witness, they accumulate. Each layer of paint is a year, a conversation, a room I once stood in and can no longer return to.
